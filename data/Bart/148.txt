Heart attacks are a leading killer of both men and women in the United States. Half of those who die do so within one hour of the start of symptoms and before reaching the hospital. Prompt treatment of a heart attack can help prevent or limit damage to the heart and prevent sudden death. Angina is a recurring pain or discomfort in the chest that happens when some part of the heart does not receive enough blood. People with angina may have a hard time telling the difference between angina and heart attack symptoms. Call 9-1-1 for an ambulance right away -- within 5 minutes. They carry drugs and equipment that can help your medical condition. Talk to your doctor about making an emergency action plan, and discuss it with your family. The emergency plan should include - warning signs or symptoms, instructions for accessing emergency medical services in your community, and steps you can take while waiting for medical help to arrive. Important information to take along with you to the hospital, including a list of medications that you take or that you are allergic to, and name and number of whom you should contact if you go to a hospital.
