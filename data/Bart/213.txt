Bell palsy is sudden weakness on one side of your face. It happens when a nerve in your face (called the facial nerve) swells and gets squeezed. This nerve is called the facial orseventh cranial nerve. Damage to this nerve causes weakness or paralysis of these muscles. Paralysis means that you cannot use the muscles at all. Symptoms vary, but may include twitching, weakness, drooping eyelid or corner of the mouth, drooling, dry eye or mouth, impairment of taste, and excessive tearing in the eye. Treatment usually involves medicine called corticosteroids. Most people with Bell palsy get better within several months, even without treatment. Surgery to relieve pressure on the nerve (decompression surgery) has not been shown to benefit most people with the disorder. Physical therapy, facial massage and acupuncture have also been used to treat Bell's palsy. Some people who have severe Bell. palsy and can't move oneSide of their face at all may not recover fully. The exact cause is unknown, but many researchers believe that a virus may lead to swelling of the 7th cranial. nerve. It may take weeks or even months for the muscles to get stronger, but most people recover completely, without treatment, in several months. The symptoms often begin to improve right away, but it may take a few weeks or months for them to get completely better. For more information, visit Bell Palsy.org.
