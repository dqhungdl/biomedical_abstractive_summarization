Elbow pain can be caused by many problems. Rest your elbow. Elevate your elbow by raising it above the level of your heart. Ice your elbow for 15 to 20 minutes at a time, 3 to 4 times a day. Treatment depends on the cause, but may involve: Antibiotics Corticosteroid shots Manipulation Pain medicine Physical therapy Surgery (last resort) is an option for people with elbow pain. The pain is due to a direct elbow injury. You have fever or swelling and redness of the elbow. Your elbow is locked and can't straighten or bend. Gently try to move the elbow and increase your range of motion. If this hurts or you cannot moving the elbow, call your health care provider. If you have heart disease, high blood pressure, kidney or liver disease, or have had stomach ulcers or internal bleeding in the past, talk with your provider before using these medicines. You can buy these pain medicines at the store. They can help with pain, but not swelling. They are available in the U.S. and Canada for about $4.99 per pill. They include ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin), or naproxen (Aleve, Naprosyn)
