Most machines and devices will not interfere with your pacemaker. Stay away from large motors, generators, and equipment. Tell all providers that you have a pacemaker before any tests are done. You will probably be able to go home after 1 day or even the same day in some cases. Ask your provider how much you can use the arm on the side of your body where the pacemaker was placed. You may be advised not to: Lift anything heavier than 10 to 15 pounds (4.5 to 6.75 kilograms) Push, pull, and twist your arm for 2 to 3 weeks. Raise your arm above your shoulder for several weeks. DO NOT lean over the open hood of a car that is running. Do not put your cell phone in a pocket on the same side of you body as yourpacemaker. Be careful around metal detectors and security wands. Handheldsecurity wands may interfere withYour pacemaker, so ask your provider about any specific device that you need to avoid. Most appliances in your home are safe to be around. You should keep several devices at least 12 inches (30 centimeters) away from the site where thepacemaker is placed under your skin. These include: Battery powered cordless tools (such as screwdrivers and drills) Plug-in power tools ( such as drills and table saws)
