Aligned with the general programme of work 2019-2023 (GPW13), the new strategy will support WHO Member States in developing seasonal influenza prevention and control capacities. WHO/SEARO/T.Pietrasik WHO is working closely with Ministries of Health, regional and national influenza research and surveillance centres, and other stakeholders to develop a multi-layered approach to preparing for and responding to both seasonal flu outbreaks and pandemics. The 2009 Swine flu A(H1N1) pandemic, started in Mexico and spread to over 214 countries and overseas territories or communities. Specific WHO programmes include the Global Influenza Surveillance and Response System (GISRS), and the Pandemic Influenza Preparedness Framework, which helps developing countries access vaccines, antivirals, and diagnostics to both prepare for and manage pandemics, as well as to prevent and control the spread of pandemic influenza in the event of a global outbreak of the H5N1 avian influenza virus (A(H5N1) or the 2009 H1N1 swine flu virus (Swine flu) virus (Pandemic influenza) in the near future, and to respond to the next pandemic.
