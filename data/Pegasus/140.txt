A person diagnosed with persistent depressive disorder may have brain episodes of major depression along with periods of less severe symptoms, but symptoms must last for two years to be considered persistent depression disorder. Winter affective disorder is characterized by the onset of depression during the winter months, when there is less natural sunlight. Depression, especially in midlife or older adults, can co-occur with other serious medical illnesses, such as diabetes, cancer, heart disease, and Parkinsons disease.
