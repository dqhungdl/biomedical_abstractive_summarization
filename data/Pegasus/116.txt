Some tips that may help reduce swelling: Put your legs on pillows to raise them above your heart while lying down. This helps pump fluid from your legs back to your heart. Follow a low-salt diet, which may reduce fluid buildup and swelling. Follow your health care provider's treatment recommendations. If you have long-term swelling, ask your provider about options to prevent skin breakdown, such as: - Flotation ring - Lamb's wool pad - Pressure-reducing mattress Continue with your everyday activities. When lying down, take breaks often to stand up and move around. Wear support stockings (sold at most drugstores and medical supply stores). Avoid wearing tight clothing or garters around your thighs. Follow the advice of your doctor and follow the treatment recommendations of your provider.
