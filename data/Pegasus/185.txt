Trigeminal neuralgia (TN), also called tic douloureux, is a chronic pain condition that causes extreme, sporadic, sudden burning or shock-like face pain. TN occurs most often in people over age 50, but it can occur at any age, and is more common in women than in men. The cause is probably a blood vessel pressing on the trigeminal nerve, one of the largest nerves in the head. The intensity of pain can typically be felt on one side of the face but in some cases it is part of the normal aging process but can also occur on the other side. TN may be caused by: Multiple sclerosis (MS) or other diseases that damage the protective myelin sheath covering myelin of the nerves Pressure on the TRIGUMinal nerve from a swollen blood vessel or tumor Injury to the trigenal nerve, such as from trauma to the face or from oral or sinus surgery Often, no exact cause is found. In the days before they recur, some patients may experience a somewhat constant pain-free period, but episodes can last for months, weeks, or even years.
