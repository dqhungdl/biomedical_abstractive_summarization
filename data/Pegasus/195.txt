Osteoarthritis usually comes with age and most often affects the fingers, knees, and hips. For example, you might have badly injured your knee when young and develop arthritis in your knee joint years later. Reactive Arthritis is pain or swelling in a joint that is caused by an infection in your body. Rheumatoid arthritis happens when the bodys own defense system doesnt work properly. For instance, you may have suffered a knee injury years ago and now you are suffering from rheumatoid arthritis, which is pain in the joints caused by inflammation of the joints. For osteoarthritis, it usually involves redness, heat, swelling, and pain in your spine or in the joint where the bottom of the spine joins the pelvic bone. Juvenile Arthritis is the term used to describe arthritis in children. Arthritis usually affects the big toe, but many other joints may be affected.
