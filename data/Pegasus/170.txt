Anticonvulsant medicines help control seizures Behavior therapy helps manage hyperactivity, sleep problems, and development problems Occupational and speech therapy manage speech problems and teach living skills Physical therapy helps with walking and movement problems Angelman Syndrome (AS) is a genetic condition that causes problems with the way a child's body and brain develop. Children with this condition typically have a small head size (epilepsy) and an abnormal side-to-side curvature of the spine (scoliosis) The life expectancy of people with this syndrome appears to be nearly normal, and the life expectancy for people without this syndrome is about 10 to 12 years, according to the National Institute of Neurological Disorders and Stroke (NINDS) (http://www.nih.gov/disorders/neurodegenerative-disorders-and-conditions/angelman-syndrome/index.html).
