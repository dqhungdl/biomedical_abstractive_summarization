The eustachian tube runs from the middle part of each ear to the back of the throat. Pain that you feel in the ear may be coming from another place, such as your teeth, the joint in your jaw (temporomandibular joint), or your throat. Ear pain in adults is less likely to be from an ear infection. Causes of ear pain may include: Arthritis of the jaw Short-term ear infection Long term ear infection Ear injury from pressure changes (from high altitudes and other causes) Oblonged ear canal irritation from cotton-tipped swabs Soap or shampoo staying in ear An earache is a sharp, dull, or burning pain in one or both ears. Related conditions include: Otitis media Swimmer's ear Malignant otitis externa For this reason, these headaches are also called medicine overuse headaches. A tension headache: - May be related to stress, depression, anxiety, a head injury, or holding your head and neck in an abnormal position. Other types of headaches: - Cluster headache is an sharp, very painful headache that occurs up to several times a day for months. Other forms of headache include: - Headaches may occur if you have a cold, the flu, a fever, or premenstrual syndrome
