The most common disorders outside the joints that cause pain around the joints include: Fibromyalgia Polymyalgia rheumatica Bursitis or tendinitis Warm baths, massage, and stretching exercises should be used as often as possible. Pain in many joints is usually caused by: Arthritis Arthritis is joint inflammation that causes swelling, pain, and stiffness in the joints. The causes of the arthritis are different depending on whether the joints started hurting suddenly (acutely) or have been hurting for a long time (chronic). Reactive (acute) arthritis in more than one joint is most often cause by: Infection from a virus The start of a joint disorder or a flare up of a long-term joint disorder (such as rheumatoid arthritis) Less common causes include: Lyme disease Gonorrhea Streptococcal infections arthritis Gout Chronic arthritis in over one joint, which is the most common cause in older adults Autoimmune disorders Juvenile idiopathic arthritis (in children) Rheumatology is a branch of the immune system that attacks the body's own tissues and organs, including the joints, muscles, tendons, ligaments, cartilage, blood vessels, nerves, and blood vessels.
