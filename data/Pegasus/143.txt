Tell your doctor if any of these symptoms are severe or do not go away: - dizziness or lightheadedness - tiredness - depression - nausea - dry mouth - stomach pain - vomiting - gas or bloating - heartburn - constipation - rash or itching - cold hands and feet - runny nose Some side effects can be serious. Most are mild, such as a stomach aches or drowsiness, and go away after you stop taking the drug. The following symptoms are uncommon, but if you experience any of them, call your doctor immediately: - shortness of breath - wheezing - swelling of the hands, feet, ankles, or lower legs - weight gain - fainting - rapid, pounding, or irregular heartbeat Metoprolol may cause other side effects. If you experience a serious side effect, you or your doctor may send a report to the Food and Drug Administration's (FDA) MedWatch Adverse Event Reporting program online (http://www.fda.gov/Safety/MedWatch) or by phone (1-800-332-1088).
