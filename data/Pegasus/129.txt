Make Sure Your Catheter Is Working : You will need to check your catheter and bag throughout the day. Drink 8 to 12 glasses of water every day for a few days after you change your catheter. Caring for Your Skin Near your Catheter : Follow these guidelines for good health and skin care: - Check the catheter site a few times a day. You may need a catheter because you have urinary incontinence (leakage), urinary retention (not being able to urinate), surgery that made a catheter necessary, or another health problem. When to Call the Doctor : Call your health care provider if: - You are having trouble changing your catheter or emptying your bag. If you need to call the doctor, you can: - Call the doctor's office if you have a urinary tract infection (UTI) - Call your doctor if your catheter is not working - Call a nurse if you are having problems with your catheter - Ask your doctor for a prescription to buy a catheter - If you have any questions about your catheter, call your doctor or a nurse at your local health care center - Ask a nurse or a doctor at a medical supply store if you need a prescription for a catheter You will also need to know how to change a catheter.
