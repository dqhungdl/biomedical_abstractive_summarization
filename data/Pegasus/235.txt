Treatment includes: Eating a healthy diet Losing weight (if you are overweight) Your health care provider may prescribe medicine if: You have already had a heart attack or stroke, or you have peripheral arterial disease Your LDL (bad) cholesterol level is 190 mg/dL or higher You are 40-75 years old, you have diabetes, and your LDL cholesterol level (probably ages 17 and 20 years, if they have risk factors for heart disease or stroke) can be detected if you eat more fatty foods, if you exercise more frequently and if your cholesterol levels are checked at a younger age. You can lower cholesterol if members of your family have it, if blood cholesterol tests are more likely to be detected with a blood cholesterol test. When deciding which medicine you should take and which dose you need, your health plan provider will consider Your cholesterol levels Your risk for life-threatening heart disease and stroke Your age Any other health problems you have Possible side effects of the medicines.
